Video Search

Check out the tool

The tool is part of my master thesis on in-video content searching and result visualization. It supports quick course video searching for students studying Visualization.

The tool is built using HTML, CSS, and Javascript. A client-side search library Lunr.js is used to enable searching within video captions. The website utilized YouTube IFrame Player API to display and control video play by interacting with the visualized results and to ensure only one video is played at a time.

Project Background

As MOOCs become increasingly popular, video learning plays a more and more important role in education. Also, during the covid-19 pandemic, many institutions have switched to distance learning, and using videos such as lecture recordings have become more common.

KTH also started distance teaching in 2020. during the autumn semester, the course Visualization was conducted remotely. In addition, the course used an approach called the flipped classroom, where students first watch several videos about visualization before each class.

In a flipped classroom, Video is an essential part because students learn all the important concepts and methods by watching videos. But we lack a video platform for all course videos in Visualization. Instead, they were distributed on different pages on Canvas, which was very inconvenient. Therefore, the project aimed to develop a one-page application for organizing all the course videos and supporting search in course content to find the accurate video chapters.
